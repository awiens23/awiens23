Past Projects

APSC 200 Design Project

Part One

The first project was a 6 week theoretical exercise based on planning a manned mission to Mars. The main constraint was all fuel would be consumed during transit, so the team had to find a way to generate fuel on planet. The first proposed solution was to use carbon dioxide, sunlight and Martian ice to grow cyanobacteria, which would in turn would create O2 to feed E. Coli bacteria that would produce 2,3-Butanediol to use as fuel. The second solution again used Martian ice, which is melted and fed into a capillary-drawn electrolyis system to seperate H2 and O2. My role in the project was to plan the logistics and conduct the economic analysis. This involved extensive research into everything needed to survive on Mars, creating a timeline from launch to return, and creating an intensive budget.

Part Two

This project centered around identifying a current inclusivity problem in today's sports industry and solving it. The team chose to redesign an archer's arm guard to be inclusive to any body type or gender. The design portion of the project included 3D modelling a lattice to best absorb shock, and then conducting Finite Element Analysis. For this project, my role was to help model and conduct the FEA using SolidWorks, as well as conduct the economic analysis of the project. This involved market research, product design, material sourcing, and pricing the project.

APSC 103 Design Project

For this project, the team was hired by the client, the Tea Room, to create a greenhouse that could operate year-round to provide the cafe with fresh produce. Given numerous constraints and a limited budget, the team designed a vertical greenhouse consisting of 3 tiers, a rainwater collection and watering system, UV lights, a heating system, a solar generator, and insulated walls. A model was also assembled to showcase the design to the client. My role in this this project was multifaceted; for the theory side of the project, I researched and modelled which vegetables should be grown, and which strains would work best. This also included implementing a complimentary planting strategy to aid in growth and yield. With the product itself, I 3D modelled the greenhouse in SolidWorks, and helped to create the physical model in the Queen's ILC workshop.
